# Resume writing best practices (project reference)

> Condensed from common 2025 guidance on ATS + human readers + AI-era hiring.
> Used to steer **this repo’s generators** and manual edits—not legal advice.

## Why resumes get rejected (or feel “AI-written”)

1. **Keyword stuffing** — Long comma lists of JD terms in the Summary. Modern ATS uses more NLP; unnatural repetition can hurt perceived quality.
2. **Generic filler** — Phrases like “passionate”, “world-class”, “synergy”, “leverage”, “robust solutions” without proof.
3. **Mismatch of evidence** — Summary claims tools the Experience section never supports.
4. **All keywords in Summary, none in bullets** — Recruiters weight **achievements in context**; bullets should carry most stack + impact.

## What works

- **Tailor per role**: Re-order projects/skills; lightly rewrite bullets to mirror JD language *where facts allow*.
- **Quantify when true**: Use numbers from KB only; never invent metrics.
- **STAR-like bullets** (when facts exist): Action → scope/tech → outcome.
- **Standard sections & parseable PDF**: Keep headings conventional; avoid hiding text in graphics.
- **Concrete over abstract**: Prefer tool names, patterns (offline-first, CI/CD), and domains (fleet, manufacturing) over buzzwords.

## This project’s implementation notes

- **Summary**: Short prose + at most a **single** natural sentence tying **a few concrete** JD terms; avoid full-keyword dumps.
- **Bold**: Limited bolding in Summary; JD match is reinforced in **Experience** and **Key Skills**.
- **Profile YAML**: Keep variants factual; generator adds AUT lead + JD tail—edit KB if tone drifts.
