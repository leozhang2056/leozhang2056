We manage 80,000 hectares of trees across the country, provide carbon credits to New Zealand's Emissions Trading Scheme . We're a 75-person organisation that combines ecology, science and technology to address  climate change - and we're growing our systems capability to match our ambitions. We're looking for a Senior Software Developer who's curious about GIS and excited by the idea of working in a domain where what you build genuinely matters. The role Your primary focus is the ongoing development and operations of Nateva's Forest and Estate Management System - a .NET/MAUI application running on Azure that integrates with our GIS mapping platform. You'll own the architecture and data-layer integration between Azure SQL and PostGIS, manage external development partners, and help translate business needs into practical technology roadmaps. You don't need to be a GIS expert coming in, but you'll quickly develop a good working understanding of our spatial systems, and that knowledge will make you a significantly more effective developer in this environment. You'll also mentor colleagues across the business in building automation capability using AI-assisted Python and R. What you'll bring  5+ years software development experience 5+ years database administration (SQL, preferably with some PostGIS exposure) Experience in .NET, Python or R Familiarity with Azure or similar cloud environments Some GIS exposure (QGIS a plus, not essential) Strong communication skills - you'll be translating between technical and operational teams A full driver's licence and willingness to travel to forests around NZ (overnight stays required)  Why Nateva You'll be joining a team of 36 Auckland-based professionals - foresters, ecologists, analysts and operations staff - who are serious about their work and the environment. The domain here is genuinely unique: our forests are actively transitioning from exotic nurse crops to permanent native forest, and the systems that support that process need to be built properly. What you build will be used daily by field staff across NZ, there's real scope for this role to grow with the organisation.
