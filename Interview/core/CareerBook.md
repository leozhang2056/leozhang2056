## 1. Job Search Strategy

### Key Data

| Fact | Implication |
|---|---|
| Only **30%** of NZ jobs are publicly advertised | 70% are in the hidden market, accessed through networking |
| **50–200+** applicants per role | Blanket-sending the same CV is ineffective |
| Employers prefer **referral hiring** | Saves time, reduces cost, increases trust |

### Two Parallel Strategies

| Passive Job Search | Active Job Search |
|---|---|
| Seek / TradeMe / LinkedIn Jobs | Referrals through networking |
| Company Careers pages | Direct email/phone/Walk-in |
| Social media following companies | LinkedIn connections with industry insiders |
| Job Alerts subscriptions | Recruitment agencies |
| | Volunteering/internships as entry points |

### Job Search Management
- Excel tracker for every application (company, role, date, status)
- Screenshot JDs (prevent expiry/disappearance)
- Use job board "Save Job" features
- No response after 1 week → polite follow-up

### Direct Employer Contact Templates

**Email:**
> Kia ora, I'm [name], a [year] [degree] student at AUT. I'm keen to get involved with [org]. I would love the opportunity to chat about potential opportunities. I'm available for [part-time/roles]. CV attached. Ngā Mihi

**Walk-in (part-time work):**
> Hi, I'm [name]. I'm a full-time student looking for part-time work. I'm studying [subject] and interested in [field]. Would you like a copy of my CV or prefer email?

### Quick Job Board Reference
Seek / Trade Me Jobs / LinkedIn Jobs / Indeed / Company Careers pages

## 3. Networking & Connections

### Network Sources
Target employers / AUT Career Events / External events Hackathons / LinkedIn / Volunteering part-time internships / Professional associations / Meetup.com / AUT Clubs / Edge & Beyond Awards

### 🖊️ Personal Tips
- **Attend local meetups regularly** — best way to meet industry people and discover company pain points
- **Volunteer for dev-related activities** (e.g. Code Club at MOTAT, open-source contributions) — counts as local experience
- **Find a personal mentor** — someone who truly knows your skill level and can give candid feedback (e.g. Harry)

### Elevator Pitch (30 seconds)

5 elements:
1. Your name
2. What you study & why
3. What you're interested in
4. **One differentiating point**
5. **Ask them a question**

### Conversation Starters

**Formal settings:** "What skills are you looking for in candidates for your graduate programme?"

**Informal:** "What advice would you give a graduate trying to enter this industry?"

**Key:** Ask open-ended questions + listen + handshake/smile/posture/enthusiasm

## 4. Personal Brand

### What is Personal Brand?
Your **Unique Selling Point** — used to express values, personality, and skills

### Five-Step Discovery
1. **Passion** — What are you passionate about?
2. **Values** — What do you value? (Growth? Stability? Team?)
3. **Aspiration** — Who/what do you admire? Why?
4. **Strengths & Weaknesses** — Strengths + weaknesses
5. **Description** — How would friends/family describe you?
→ Combine into a one-sentence USP

### Display Channels
- Online: LinkedIn / GitHub / Personal website
- Appearance: First impression
- Verbal: Elevator Pitch

## 5. NZ Workplace Culture

### Company Characteristics
- Average **13 people**, flat structure
- Everyone is a generalist, independent work
- Can-do attitude valued

### Management Style
- Informal, first-name basis
- Manager is a team member
- Encouraged to contribute ideas and feedback
- **Not sure? Ask!** Don't guess on your own

### Workplace Socializing
- Morning tea, birthdays, After work drinks
- Part of building bonds and networks
- **Not a waste of time**

### Soft Skills Employers Value
Communication / Teamwork / Curiosity / Collaboration / Critical Thinking / Problem Solving / Customer Focus / Adaptability

### Why Networking is Especially Important in NZ
- Small companies → hire based on trust
- 70% hidden market

### Local Experience
NZ employers value local experience because: NZ referee + understanding workplace culture + professional skills
**Volunteer experience = local experience**

## 7. NZ Hiring Cycles

- **Feb – Mar** — New Year hiring peak
- **Mar – Apr** — Graduate / Internship openings concentrated (published one year in advance)
- **Jun – Jul** — Summer internship hiring
- **Sep – Oct** — Summer jobs & internship second wave
- **Sep – Nov** — Year-end/New Year start roles
- **Off-peak** → Prepare CV, interview prep, projects, networking

### 🖊️ Personal Observation
- **July-August** has the most openings — this is the window to go all-in
- **September-October** openings drop significantly — don't wait; start early and batch applications in Jul-Aug

## 8. CV & Cover Letter

### Employer Data (NZ 2024)
- **80%** of employers want a Cover Letter, **and they read it**
- Ideal length: **one page**
- **68%** want CL as an email attachment

### Employer Quotes
> "The key thing I want is **why you are applying specifically for this role**."
> "Don't use AI — it's really obvious."
> "A cover letter repeating the CV is a waste of time."

### CL Structure
| Opening | What you're applying for + why this company |
| Evidence | How your skills match the JD |
| Closing | Looking forward to connecting |

### CV 8 Sections
1. Contact Info
2. Career Objective
3. Skills Summary (Transferable + Technical)
4. Education
5. Work Experience (Relevant / Other)
6. Volunteering & Extracurricular
7. Achievements & Interests
8. Referees ("Available upon request")

### Common Action Verbs
Achieved / Built / Delivered / Designed / Engineered / Implemented / Integrated / Launched / Led / Managed / Optimised / Orchestrated / Reduced / Refactored / Resolved / Streamlined

## 11. Key Challenge Strategies (Mentor Supplement)

### Overqualified Issue
NZ employers are extremely focused on ROI and Retention. Senior talent applying for junior roles get rejected as over-qualified. Response: Look for mid-level roles with slightly higher years required; adjust salary slightly but not too low.

### Industry Pain Point Discovery
Understand local company technical/management pain points through Meetup; demonstrate targeted solutions in Cover Letter or interviews.

### Communication & Accent
If you don't understand, always ask the other person to rephrase — never pretend to understand. In-person interviews can use body language and whiteboard demonstrations.

### Certification Value
Bonus items, not must-haves. Core: Master professional terminology and business logic through learning, demonstrate continuous learning attitude.

### Material Self-Check Checklist
1. Are CV/LinkedIn/GitHub personal goals and core skills highly aligned?
2. Do all work descriptions follow "action + scenario + quantified results"?
3. Has LinkedIn recently had genuine (non-AI) industry opinion output?
4. Does Cover Letter reflect deep alignment with company values?

## 12. Interview Mindset Notes (from Chinese notes)

### Employer Perspective
- Employers look at: what problems you solved, scale and complexity, business value translation ability
- LinkedIn headline = value proposition + New Zealand + career drive
- GitHub README needs visual impact, letting people quickly understand projects
- Cover Letter must demonstrate understanding and insight about the company, don't overlap with CV
- Continuous learning evidence: certifications, Meetups, community engagement
- Don't pile on tech — reflect business value

### CV Strategy
- Highlight priorities, don't overfill; delete low-quality info, leave space for evidence
- Don't use vague expressions, support with specific numbers
- Save target role links for easy follow-up

### Interview Mindset
- Positive, optimistic, enthusiastic — state matters more than script
- Reverse question phase: ease the pace, demonstrate depth of thinking
- What's tested is logical thinking and expression ability, not memorization ability

### 🖊️ Personal Mindset Notes
- **Mindset matters most** — personal statement should reflect your **personality** and a **growth mindset**, not just a skill list
- Target: **intern / graduate role** — don't aim too high too fast, build from solid foundation
- **Referral strategy:** ask people who **truly know your ability** — a lukewarm referral is worse than none

### 🖊️ Technical Skill Building
| Focus | Why |
|---|---|
| **C# projects** | Widely used in NZ enterprise — adds local market relevance |
| **AI agent + full auto-pipeline** | Learn to make AI agents run code end-to-end autonomously |
| **Full-stack website + API design / MCP** | Build real web projects and design APIs / MCP servers |
| **Agent & skill authoring** | Understand how to write good agents and skills — key differentiator |
| **Harness CD platform** | Get comfortable with Harness for CI/CD — used by several NZ employers |
